Extraction Methods
The most efficient ways of extracting CBD oil are hydrocarbon (solvent) and carbon dioxide (CO2) extraction. While hydrocarbon extraction is cost-effective compared to CO2 extraction, the process uses concerning solvents to separate the oil from the hemp plants, leaving behind chemical residuals.
This leaves us with CO2 extraction as the cleanest and most-efficient option. The CO2 extraction method uses compressed liquid carbon dioxide to pull the cannabidiol (CBD) compound from hemp plants. The CO2 then evaporates, leaving behind pure CBD oil that isn't tarnished by cheap solvents.
We looked for brands that exclusively use CO2 extraction because of their ability to produce a clean, safe product. It may be the most expensive extraction process, but it's worth it for peace of mind.
Extract Types
There are three types of CBD extracts: full-spectrum, broad-spectrum, and isolate. They're all effective forms of CBD, but they each possess distinct qualities that make them attractive to various consumers for different reasons.
Full-Spectrum
Full-spectrum CBD oil contains a complete range of cannabinoids, including tetrahydrocannabinol (THC). When you ingest two or more cannabinoids, they boost each other's efficacy and create an enhanced consumer experience known as the "entourage effect."
The federal government requires that CBD products contain no more than 0.3% THC. This amount of THC is there to boost full-spectrum products and it is not enough to get your dog "high"â€“CBD products are non-psychoactive.
Broad-Spectrum
Broad-spectrum CBD oil is very similar to full-spectrum since it also contains a wide range of cannabinoids, but it has gone through a process to remove all THC. While the 0.3% or less is not enough to induce "high," some people prefer to avoid THC altogether.
Isolate
Isolate is the purest form of CBD, having gone through a process to remove all other cannabinoids (including THC). While this product does not produce the entourage effect, it does offer the most trustworthy CBD experience, uninhibited by any other compounds.
