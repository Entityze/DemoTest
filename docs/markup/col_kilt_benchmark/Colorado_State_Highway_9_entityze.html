State Highway 94 is an long east -- west Colorado state highway that goes east beginning from just east of and ending at / in rural Cheyenne County west of Wild Horse . The highway serves Schriever Air Force Base and the towns of Ellicott , Yoder , and Rush , where it then crosses through rural country . It also serves the Punkin Center area where it comes to a junction with SH 71 . It then passes through more very rural country until it reaches a stop at US 40 / 287 west of the unincorporated town of Wild Horse . Throughout most of its length , the highway runs due east -- west , pointing exactly at Pikes Peak . Under good conditions , the peak can be seen as far east as the rise between s 78 and 79 . " Garbage Hill " gets its name from the Colorado Springs landfill located north of the highway . Originally , the entrance to the landfill was partway up the hill , but currently the entrance is from Blaney Road at the top of the hill . At the bottom of the hill , which is the eastern side of Jimmy Camp Creek valley , there are wrecking yard s and two motocross tracks .
