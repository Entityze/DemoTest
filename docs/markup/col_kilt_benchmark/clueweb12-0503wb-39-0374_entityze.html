Location of the business matters only in certain industries that depend on cheap , unskilled labor . If you are producing clothing or furniture , it makes sense to have a factory in the Third World . But if you are producing sophisticated products for which labor is a small part of the cost -- or selling information or advice -- you can do it from the U.S. Information and advice is especially good because the brute labor cost is almost non-existent . Thanks to the Internet , the cost of storage and shipping is minimal , so you can easily produce your product here and sell it anywhere ... anywhere in the world .
