, was a bureaucrat and cabinet minister in the Empire of Japan , serving as Minister of Finance , and Minister of Greater East Asia . Aoki was born to a farming family in Sarashina District , ( now part of the city of Nagano ) , and was trained as a lawyer , graduating from the Law School of in 1916 . On graduation , he entered the Ministry of Finance . Rising rapidly through the ranks , Aoki became chief of the Financial Bureau under Takahashi Korekiyo , who made use of his legal background to have Aoki draft a Foreign Exchange Management Act , which was passed by the in 1933 . Up until that time , Japan had not attempted to implement comprehensive state control over foreign exchange . Aoki followed up on this law with the Rice Control Act ( 1933 ) and the Petroleum Control Act ( 1934 ) , which set the stage for increasing state control over strategic sectors of the economy . He was also on the committee which drafted the National Service Draft Ordinance , which placed the Japanese economy on a war economy footing after the start of the Second Sino-Japanese War . Prime Minister Fumimaro Konoe asked Aoki to become deputy director of the in 1937 , and he became its chairman in 1939 . The same year , Aoki was nominated to a seat in the Upper House in the Diet . Under Prime Minister Abe , Aoki was Minister of Finance in 1939 , while retaining his post as chairman of the Planning Board . After the fall of the Abe administration , Aoki was assigned as a special envoy to the Reorganized National Government of China to guide economic policy . He was recalled to Japan under the to the newly-created cabinet post of Minister of Greater East Asia , in which position he oversaw the Greater East Asia Conference . After the end of World War II , Aoki was arrested ( as were all former government members ) by the and held in Sugamo Prison on charges of war crime s. However , he was released in 1948 without coming to trial . Afterwards , Aoki established a private legal practice . In 1953 , he ran for a seat in the with the support of the Liberal Party ( Japan , 1945 ) on a nation-wide ticket . He was subsequently reelected to the same seat three more times as a member of the Liberal Democratic Party ( LDP ) . He joined a right-wing faction within the LDP in 1960 which was adamantly opposed to Japan ’s normalization of relations with the and supported Japan ’s continued recognition of the on Taiwan . He was also a strong supporter of building a nation-wide network of highways in Japan , especially the Chūō Expressway . After his retirement from politics , Aoki joined the Board of Directors of Shin-etsu Broadcasting , and in 1968 was one of the founders of the Nagano Broadcasting Systems . In 1971 , he was awarded the Order of the Rising Sun
