Whitesands Bay ( ) shown as Whitesand Bay on some maps , is an award-winning , standard , wide sandy beach in St Brides Bay in the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park , United Kingdom . The beach is located about two miles west of the small city of and about one mile south of and has been described as the best surfing beach in Pembrokeshire and one of the best tourist beaches in the world . The area to the north east of the bay is dominated by a large rocky outcrop , at its highest point , known as Carn Llidi - which could mean Cairn of the Gates or Cairn of Wrath . The Pembrokeshire Coast Path passes alongside the bay , giving access in the north to the secluded bays of Porthlleuog and Porthmelgan ( which are only accessible on foot ) , and the rugged coastal scenery of St Davids Head . To the south , the coastal path leads to Porthselau and St. Justinian 's , with views across the Ramsey Sound to Ramsey Island .
