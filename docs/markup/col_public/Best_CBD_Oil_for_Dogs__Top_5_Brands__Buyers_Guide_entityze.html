The Types of Extracts
CBD oils are made into three primary extracts.
Although there are certain DIY methods that people have been using for some time now, the most common extracts you'll see advertised include:
Isolate
Isolate is when all of the components of the hemp plant are stripped to leave just the CBD. This method is popular because it contains 0% THC (the psychoactive component in marijuana that gets you 'high'), as well as gives an experience some people prefer in containing only CBD.
Broad Spectrum
Broad spectrum is a minimally processed version of CBD, containing less than 0.3% THC. It also often hosts other parts of the cannabis plant giving off an 'entourage effect', which many believe heightens the experience. This is a good in between of isolate and full spectrum, which is the least processed version of CBD.
Full Spectrum
Finally, full spectrum CBD is the least processed and contains the most original plant particles. Cited as the strongest entourage effect, full spectrum oils are used by CBD enthusiasts who want the most out of their product. Like broad spectrum oils, full spectrum varieties also contain less than 0.3% THC.
Potency
The potency of a CBD product is almost always measured in milligrams (mg). This is essentially the strength of the product, where the higher the milligram per volume, the stronger the dosage will be. The same is true for other ingredients often found in CBD oils such as CBG or melatonin (which helps humans sleep).
For your dog, the milligram will be determined by their weight. Most brands with dog specific products will recommend based on small, medium, or large breeds. Some exceptions do occur, such as with dogs who have particularly painful chronic conditions. Consult with your vet if you're going to be giving you dog CBD daily or on a regular basis.
Lab Results
As we stated above, any CBD oil company worth taking is going to include their lab results. Companies that don't have their lab results, or their lab results are not updated within the past year, should be knocked out of your consideration (especially since this is something you're taking yourself or giving to your dog). Furthermore, it can sometimes be a smart idea to back check the lab that they used for testing to ensure that the results weren't forged.
Size Variations
CBD oils often come in a few different sizes including 150 mg, 300 mg, 600 mg or up to 1500 mg in some cases for dogs (however, the first three are the most common). The sizes are done by breed size, unless of an exception.

What Does CBD Oil Do For Dogs?
Like humans, dogs have an endocannabinoid system similar to humans. This can help with managing many of the symptoms humans experience too like anxiety, pain, and other neurological or physical issues. In less extreme cases, CBD oils can also help dogs with things like falling asleep or increasing appetite.
For most dog owners, CBD is an occasional treatment used to help with extra aggrevating or anxious times (such as before bed or if there’s a loud noise like thunder or fireworks). Regular usage is normal amongst those with dogs who suffer from chronic pain or anxiety, especially when an owner leaves for work; or if the dog was previously in an abusive situation. Do some research and consult with your vet before prescribing CBD as a daily treatment.
Eliminate Pain
One issue that CBD oil helps with in both humans and dogs is helping with inflammation and reducing pain. For people who use over the counter medications like aspirin daily, CBD has been a good replacement considering it contains less toxicity. This is also true for dogs, where pain medication is limited to access. A great alternative for dogs with joint or chronic pain, CBD oil’s word of mouth has only spread since its introduction to the mainstream.
Reduce Anxiety
For dogs that suffer from anxiety, CBD oil has become a great item for owners to have on hand. Especially in places that have a lot of thunderstorms or those who have to leave several hours for work sometimes, CBD oil has been able to significantly reduce anxiety. This is considered true because CBD oil has anxiolytic, which hosts anti-anxiety and anti-inflammatory properties. An overall great alternative for both situational and regular anxiety, CBD oil for dogs has been a lifesaver for some in helping their dog live a worry free life.
Is CBD Safe for My Dog to Take?
CBD is safe for all animals. Often recommended by veterinarians as a low cost alternative to other medications, CBD oil has been promising. Perhaps the worst case scenario if your pet has taken too much CBD is that they’ll fall asleep, vomit, or have diarrhea. However, when properly administered, these situations are rare. Obviously, if your dog has a chronic condition, it’s always best to check with your veterinarian beforehand.
How Much Should I Give My Dog?
Dosages are determined by the size of the dog.
The general rule of thumb is to start with 1-2mg for every 10 pounds.
So, if your dog is 50 pounds, then 50-100mg would be suffice.
While going slightly over won’t necessarily harm your dog, they’ll most likely fall asleep or be lethargic. Double check to see if there are any breed-specific requirements for CBD before administering to your dog.
Always Examine The Ingredients
The product quality of your CBD is going to be a huge determining factor on if it’s worth your dog taking or not.
In ranking the hierarchy of needs for CBD oil ingredients, the first should be that they come from an all natural or organic source. Second, make sure that they didn’t use any unnecessary chemicals in the cultivation process. You’ll also want to check the ingredients for any excess filler. And finally, always make sure the hemp came from a reputable source and didn’t have to travel far to be manufactured.
Another piece of note for ingredients is that they often include things like vitamins or other enhancements for your pet. These are actually a good thing, as it often says the person making the product has put some time and effort into making something pet lovers will enjoy. Be on the lookout for companies like this, as their design and dedication is worth paying attention to. Regardless of the price, quality CBD is made by those who want to see the industry grow and thrive. That starts with giving people an honest product made with integrity, where that journey starts at the ingredients.
Should I Talk With My Veterinarian Before Giving My Dog CBD?
While you might not need their recommendation if you’re simply using CBD sparingly with your dog, it’s never a bad idea to speak with a professional before administering. Especially if you view CBD as something your dog will take every day, it’s good to double check it won’t interfere with any current conditions, or if there are any extra steps you should prepare for (such as taking the dog for walks more often to combat lethargy). As CBD has been studied quite a bit by experts, it’s still a relatively new field, and as such, should be taken with a grain of patience and learning. Ultimately, the goal is to create a better life with this product, which will take a little effort.
